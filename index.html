Project

Overview

I intend to build a social platform that has one key difference than the already saturated marketplace of social media [9]. My application won't support text or image posts and will instead force users to submit either audio or video posts to their profile.

This application will be cross-platform and feature a message-context border for posts. Users will be able to choose the “tone” of recordings from an “emoji wheel” that corresponds to the main emotion the recording conveys.

Audio posts will be the default mode of submission and for good reason. This initial research suggests that audio books are better at eliciting emotion than films [10]. I also believe it will be more natural to make a quick audio post than a video recording.

Motivation

I'm passionate about people, relationships, and human connection, so I wanted to create a multi-platform app that can add another dimension to the online experience of social media. I came to realise that the prime difference between social media platforms is the default medium of expression and the restrictions surrounding this.

For example, Twitter enforces a character limit of 280 characters [11]. This results in frequent and short posts that read like a stream-of-consciousness, which encourages users to share their thoughts as they occur. It's the inherent rules built into a platform that determine the mode of social expression.

However, I feel like regardless of whether an application forces the user to write a message of a certain length or post a picture, these mediums are lacking the emotional depth of face-to-face human communication. This can best be seen in the platitude “93% of communication is non-verbal”; importantly, this figure was originally derived by combining 55% body language and 38% tone of voice that make up the total [12].

What this means for social media is that text-based messages and emoticons are an inadequate form of expression. How I intend to solve this problem is to make a platform where text-based messages aren't supported, which forces the user to either send voice messages or short videos in the exact same way Twitter's character limit forces people to share bite-sized ideas.

Description

Users join the platform by entering their real name, password, and email address into a standard sign up form. The app is usable at this point, and the next screen is a profile page. The ability to personalise the profile by adding a picture and biography is locked until the user confirms their email address. In a world where our attention span is nearing that of a goldfish [13], it's important that the app is immediately usable and provides users with the option of doing clerical tasks later.

The next view is a minimal dark-themed UI that is the user's page where their posts display in a masonry-style tiled layout. At the bottom of this page is two floating icons: a microphone and a video camera. Users can begin recording in audio or video by choosing the appropriate icon. At this point, the icon expands to bring up an overlay that shows the length of the recording, a preview button, an emoji button, and a post button that is unusable until the user selects an appropriate emoji for the recording.

At the top of the user's page is a floating menu that can take user's to their feed, a view that shows the content of everyone they follow, and a link to the profile page. The feed's content is playable directly from this page, but a replies link underneath, showing the total number of replies per post, expands into a thread-style interface where users can reply to one another's content.

There is no like feature on this platform in order to inspire legitimate interaction.

Imagine recording a quick 20 second audio update about your day, which went well. You select one of the many positive emojis from the emoji button inside the recording tab, which adds a green border around the finished post on the user's page. This is the equivalent of captioning a photo on another platform, and right away, the audience in your social platform can see you just made a positive post.

This brief but incomplete insight into the context of the recording adds a dimension of personality to the user's profile and also drives curiosity. “I wonder what that happy-looking recording is about?” Key-frame video previews are intentionally hidden for this reason. At a higher level, this feature also acts as a gate keeper and content filter.

We all have that friend who largely posts negative content, and this can be overwhelming. Maybe a user has a preference for only following people with green, happy feeds. Likewise, when we're in a dark place, it can be beneficial to engage with people having similar experiences, and a blue feed would quickly enable someone to convey they're not okay to friends and families without coming out and directly saying it.

Finally, this application is accessible by default to those with reading and writing problems and those with vision impairment. And because audio recording are the default mode of submission, this platform could potentially be amazing for musicians and artists. I see this platform as being a better digital representation of socialising.

This application is built using the core web technologies of HTML5, CSS3, and ECMAScript 10. These technologies get consolidated using Electron.js to become a desktop application that can also run in the browser [14].

The front end uses React.js, Redux, and SCSS.

The back end uses Node.js, Express, and MongoDB.

For the mobile version of this application, React Native will be used [15].

Outcome

This platform will humanise the impersonal state of digital socialising by adding the emotional depth of inflections, expressions, tone of voice, gestures, and all manner of micro communications in a way that texts and still-frame snapshots could never compete against.

The built-in content filtering of the context border will ideally provide a healthier online social experience without the meaningless exchange of validation in the form of likes, upvotes, and hearts. Content will be more interactive and personal by default after removing content previews that allow users to skim rather than engage.

Accessibility for vision impaired users is a considerable advantage as well as creating a space for musicians and artists to share their work. The sum of this application's parts will be a social experience that will disrupt the social economy of validation in favour of personal content presented in a minimal UI that's free of the feature clutter associated with older established platforms.

The overall impact is a social media experience that better fits human communication.
